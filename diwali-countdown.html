What is Diwali?

Calculate this tool using proven mathematical methods. The tool above processes your inputs and returns results with appropriate precision.

The Five Days of Diwali

Day 1 - Dhanteras: The festival begins with Dhanteras, dedicated to Goddess Lakshmi and Lord Dhanvantari. People buy gold, silver, and new utensils for prosperity.

Day 2 - Naraka Chaturdashi (Choti Diwali): Celebrates Lord Krishna's victory over demon Narakasura. People wake early for ritual oil baths and light diyas at dawn.

Day 3 - Diwali (Lakshmi Puja): The main day! Homes are decorated with diyas and rangoli. Lakshmi Puja is performed in the evening for wealth and prosperity.

Day 4 - Govardhan Puja/Padwa: Celebrates Krishna lifting Mount Govardhan. In some regions, it marks the new year and celebrates husband-wife bonds.

Day 5 - Bhai Dooj: Sisters pray for their brothers' well-being and apply tilak on their foreheads. Brothers give gifts in return.

ü™î Significance of Diyas

Lighting diyas (oil lamps) during Diwali represents the triumph of light over darkness. Traditionally, homes light diyas to welcome Goddess Lakshmi and guide ancestors' spirits. The flame symbolizes knowledge dispelling ignorance.

Diwali Traditions

  • Rangoli: Colorful floor designs made with colored powders, flowers, or rice
  • Lakshmi Puja: Evening prayers to Goddess Lakshmi for prosperity
  • Fireworks: Lighting firecrackers to celebrate (eco-friendly alternatives encouraged)
  • Sweets: Exchanging and sharing mithai (Indian sweets)
  • New Clothes: Wearing new clothes, especially traditional attire
  • Cleaning: Deep cleaning homes before Diwali (like spring cleaning)
  • Gambling: Card games on Diwali night are considered auspicious

Popular Diwali Foods

  • Gulab Jamun: Sweet milk dumplings in sugar syrup
  • Kaju Katli: Diamond-shaped cashew fudge
  • Ladoo: Round sweet balls (besan, motichoor, boondi)
  • Barfi: Milk-based fudge in various flavors
  • Jalebi: Crispy, syrup-soaked spirals
  • Chakli/Murukku: Savory spiral snacks
  • Namak Pare: Salty diamond-shaped snacks
