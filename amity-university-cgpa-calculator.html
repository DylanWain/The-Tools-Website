Writing Numbers in Words

When to Spell Out Numbers

Different style guides have varying rules for when to write numbers as words versus numerals. Generally accepted conventions include spelling out numbers zero through nine (or sometimes ninety-nine), numbers that begin sentences, and approximations or round numbers.

Legal and financial documents often require amounts written in words to prevent fraud and ensure clarity. Checks, contracts, and official documents frequently include both numeral and word forms for verification.

Number Naming Conventions

English uses the short scale for large numbers, where each new term (million, billion, trillion) represents 1,000 times the previous. Some European languages use the long scale, where billion means million million. This can cause confusion in international contexts.

The short scale progression: thousand (10¬≥), million (10‚Å∂), billion (10‚Åπ), trillion (10¬π¬≤), quadrillion (10¬π‚Åµ), and so on.

Writing Checks and Legal Documents

  • Write the amount in words on the line provided
  • Use "and" only before the cents (e.g., "One hundred twenty-three and 45/100")
  • Draw a line after the amount to prevent additions
  • Ensure words match the numeral amount exactly
  • Use hyphens for compound numbers (twenty-one through ninety-nine)

International Variations

Number words vary across English dialects. British English uses "and" more frequently (e.g., "one hundred and twenty-three"), while American English often omits it. Currency names and formats also differ internationally.
